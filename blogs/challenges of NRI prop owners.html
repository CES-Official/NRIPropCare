Challenges Faced by NRI Property Owners in Renting Their Properties

Challenges and their Solutions

Posted by NRI Property Care on December 1, 2024

Owning property in India while residing abroad is a common scenario for the majority of Non-Resident Indians (NRIs). However, managing and renting out these properties can present a host of unique challenges that often become a source of stress and financial risk. Reports indicate that as much as 40% of NRIs do not put their property on rent, and they not only lose monthly rental income, but vacant property generally deteriorates, and needs fixing before it becomes habitable, plus paying property taxes from pocket.

Let us take a closer look at the hurdles NRI property owners frequently encounter when renting their properties and how can they overcome these hurdles with ease:

  1. Limited Physical Access
    One of the biggest challenges for NRIs is the inability to physically oversee their property. Tasks such as showing the property to potential tenants, conducting inspections, or addressing maintenance issues become difficult to manage from abroad. This lack of physical presence often leads to delays in resolving problems and a higher dependency on local contacts, follow-ups, much higher expenses, and frustration at the end of the day.
  2. Difficulty in Finding Reliable Tenants
    Tenant screening is critical but challenging for NRIs. Without proper checks, they risk renting their property to tenants who may default on rent, damage the property, or violate lease terms. Conducting background checks, police verifications, and assessing tenant reliability is often a cumbersome process for those not in the country.
  3. Risks of Tenant Misuse and Legal Issues
    Anyone over 18 living in the property should undergo thorough screening, including background checks, and rental histories. For applicants with guarantors or co-signers, verify their financial and legal standing as well.
  4. Lack of Transparency in Maintenance and Repairs
    Ensuring regular maintenance and timely repairs is a significant challenge. Many NRIs struggle to find trustworthy contractors and vendors to handle repairs without supervision. Moreover, thereâ€™s a risk of being overcharged or receiving subpar services, leading to increased expenses. Contractor delays are a common thing that happens in India.
  5. Time Zone and Communication Barriers
    Managing tenant queries, maintenance requests, or legal issues often involves real-time communication. With NRIs living in different time zones, and their busy time schedules, addressing these concerns promptly becomes a logistical nightmare. Delayed responses can lead to tenant dissatisfaction or unresolved issues escalating over time, which may result to rental payments being delayed etc.
  6. Fear of Encroachment or Illegal Occupancy
    Many NRIs worry about their property being encroached upon, especially when it remains vacant for extended periods. Illegal occupancy or disputes over property boundaries are real risks, and tackling such situations from afar can be both stressful and expensive. Further unauthorized subletting of property is also a cause of concern.
  7. Managing Financial and Tax Implications
    NRIs often face challenges in collecting rent and managing the financial aspects of property ownership, including taxes. Issues like delayed rent payments, converting rental income into foreign currency, and complying with Indian tax laws can add to the complexity.
  8. Dependence on Friends or Family
    In the absence of a professional property management service, NRIs often rely on friends or family to oversee their property. This arrangement can often strain relationships and may not always guarantee the level of attention or professionalism required for effective management.
